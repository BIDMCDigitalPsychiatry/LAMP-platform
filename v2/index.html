mindLAMP

mindLAMP

Digital Health, Measured & Accessible. mindLAMP helps researchers, clinicians, and patients measure, understand, and support brain and behavioral health — spanning mental health, cognition, and neurology — anywhere, anytime.

Explore on GitHubWatch Demo

The Challenge

Measurement

Conditions affecting the brain and behavior — including mental health, cognition, and neurological disorders — are often assessed through self-report questionnaires, interviews, and clinician observations. These methods, while valuable, can be inconsistent, infrequent, and influenced by recall or response bias. Symptoms frequently fluctuate daily or even hourly, yet traditional clinic visits capture only isolated “snapshots” of experience. Critical patterns and warning signs can go undetected.

Access to Care

Access to care is constrained by multiple systemic barriers: provider shortages and high demand create long wait times; high costs and lack of insurance coverage limit access; stigma discourages many from seeking support; and geographic barriers — especially in rural or underserved areas — make in-person treatment difficult. These challenges leave many without the care or data they need.

The Solution: mindLAMP

mindLAMP is an open‑source digital health platform designed to support a wide range of use cases in psychiatry, psychology, neurology, and cognitive science. It addresses challenges of both measurement and access to care.

  • Better Measurement: Capture real‑time, high‑resolution insights through smartphone sensors, surveys, and cognitive tasks.
  • Improved Access: Provide support, psychoeducation, and monitoring directly through a smartphone.
  • Research–Clinical Synergy: Already deployed in clinics, ensuring innovations translate directly into patient care.

Today, mindLAMP is listed in the World Health Organization Digital Health Atlas, used worldwide by research groups, and implemented in busy community clinics, including Harvard Medical School teaching hospitals.

Key Features

Measurement Tools

  • Digital Phenotyping: Collect granular data about behaviors and symptoms via smartphone and connected‑device sensors across operating systems. The Cortex pipeline helps teams (1) assess data quality in real time, (2) derive replicable clinical features (e.g., mobility, sleep), and (3) generate easy‑to‑share visualizations. Example: computing hometime from thousands of GPS points.

  • Ecological Momentary Assessment (EMA): Monitor behaviors and symptoms in real time, including metadata about app engagement.

  • Customizable Surveys: Configure text, icons, schedules, and notifications to capture the data you need.

  • Cognition: A suite of validated cognitive tasks studied across mental health (e.g., depression, schizophrenia) and neurological conditions (e.g., Parkinson’s, Alzheimer’s), supporting research and clinical applications.

Clinical & Research Support

  • Dashboard: Design activities, deliver surveys, visualize near‑real‑time data, and explore comprehensive datasets via the Data Portal.

  • Flexible Protocols: Run common study designs and add site‑specific questions or schedules as needed.

  • Distributed Deployment: Store data centrally or locally to comply with local, state, federal, and international laws; optionally deploy from a central site for teams not wishing to manage infrastructure.

Patient Engagement

  • Mobile App (iOS & Android): Surveys, cognitive games, meditation, breathing exercises, psychoeducation, and stress‑management resources.

  • Optional Passive Sensing: Accelerometer, GPS, pedometer, and more — running unobtrusively in the background when enabled.

  • Care Team Connection: Optional in‑app messaging and clinician dashboards to connect patients and care teams.

  • Flexible Hosting: Secure upload to servers owned by your organization (or hosted by CORE). All content and sensors can be fully customized and toggled on/off to fit use cases ranging from medication reminders to full digital‑phenotyping suites.

Platform Components

① Dashboard

Create, customize, and schedule activities; deliver notifications and interventions; access near‑real‑time data; and use the Data Portal for in‑depth exploration of multi‑modal data. The CORE team can support custom dashboards and reporting or help you use the flexible default.

② App

Participants complete surveys, cognitive games, mindfulness activities, and psychoeducation; optional background sensing collects data without disruption. Data are securely uploaded to servers you control (or hosted by CORE). The app can be adapted for use cases from simple reminders to comprehensive digital phenotyping.

③ Database

A secure, chronological index of all data built on the LAMP Protocol. It enables reproducible, shareable science and real‑time triggers for custom analyses. Host locally or have CORE support a private, secure deployment.

④ Cortex

An open‑source pipeline that connects to the database to derive clinically meaningful features (e.g., sedentary behavior, hometime, device usage, sleep estimates), produce interactive visualizations, and support just‑in‑time adaptive interventions. Run default or custom analyses per your needs.

Open Source & Community

mindLAMP is fully open source and actively maintained. The codebase is available on GitHub, and research teams worldwide are building adapted versions. This transparent, collaborative ecosystem ensures research outputs become real‑world tools that are replicable, customizable, and scalable.
